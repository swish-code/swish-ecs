# IBE Manager Key Questions

This is a concise discussion guide for the IBE manager. It focuses only on business understanding, workflow clarity, ownership, and system direction.

## 1. Business Purpose

1. What is the main business purpose of this platform for Swish?
2. What problem is the system expected to solve first?
3. Which area is the highest priority in phase 1: SOPs, compliance audits, corrective actions, or KPI validation?
4. What would make the IBE team say the project is successful?

## 2. Scope And Rollout

1. Which business areas must be covered first: Food Safety, HSE, Legal, ISO, Catering/Logistics, Environmental, KPI, or all?
2. Should the first rollout cover the whole company or only selected departments/brands?
3. Which departments or user groups should be pilot users first?
4. What should be left for later phases so phase 1 stays realistic?

## 3. Users And Ownership

1. Who are the main user groups in the system?
2. Who will own SOP creation and maintenance?
3. Who will approve SOPs?
4. Who will monitor implementation?
5. Who will perform audits?
6. Who will validate KPI updates?
7. Who will have company-wide visibility?

## 4. SOP Workflow

1. What is the expected end-to-end SOP lifecycle from draft to active use?
2. Which steps in the SOP workflow are mandatory?
3. Should all employees be able to view active SOPs?
4. Should draft or under-review SOPs be restricted to certain users only?
5. What is the review cycle for SOPs after activation?
6. What is the most important information the system must show for each SOP?

## 5. Implementation Tracking

1. After an SOP is approved, how should rollout happen in the business?
2. Who is responsible for confirming implementation?
3. How should implementation be tracked: by department, by location, by employee, or a mix?
4. What proof or evidence is needed to confirm implementation?
5. What should happen when implementation is delayed or incomplete?

## 6. Audit And Corrective Action Workflow

1. What types of audits should the system support first?
2. How are audits currently performed today?
3. What should happen when an audit finding is raised?
4. How should corrective actions be assigned, followed up, and closed?
5. What information does management need to see about audit status and overdue actions?

## 7. KPI Workflow

1. How should KPI tracking fit into this platform from a business perspective?
2. Is KPI validation as important as SOP and audit workflows, or should it come in a later phase?
3. How are KPIs assigned today: by employee, by department, or both?
4. Who updates KPI progress, and who validates it?
5. What should happen when KPI updates are missing, late, or unclear?
6. How should KPI scoring work at a business level?

## 8. Communication And Follow-Up

1. When IBE asks a question such as "Why is this KPI not updated?", how should that interaction work inside the system?
2. Should the system keep a full conversation history between IBE and users?
3. Should that communication style be used only for KPI validation, or also for audits and corrective actions?
4. What types of reminders or escalations are business-critical?

## 9. Visibility, Control, And Governance

1. What should every employee be able to see in the system?
2. What should only IBE or managers be able to see?
3. Are there records that must be tightly restricted, such as drafts, approvals, or validation decisions?
4. What level of control or governance is most important: visibility, accountability, auditability, or enforcement?

## 10. Reporting And Management Expectations

1. What are the most important dashboards or reports management expects from this platform?
2. What does the IBE manager want to see daily, weekly, and monthly?
3. Which three to five metrics matter most for leadership?
4. What would make the system truly useful for decision-making rather than only record-keeping?

## 11. Final Direction Questions

1. Should this platform remain one centralized enterprise system for SOP, compliance, audit, and KPI workflows?
2. Which module must be strongest at launch to earn trust from the business?
3. What are the biggest risks if we design the system incorrectly?
4. What decisions should be finalized first before detailed module implementation continues?

## Suggested Discussion Flow

Use this order in the meeting:

1. business purpose and success
2. scope and rollout priority
3. SOP workflow
4. implementation and audit flow
5. KPI and communication flow
6. visibility, governance, and reporting
7. final decisions and next steps
